## Lessons Learned

1. **Code review is valid QA for server-side logic.** When you can't get a live session, reading the route handler tells you exactly what happens. Magic bytes check? Read the bytes array comparison. Rate limiting? Read the counter logic. Not a substitute for E2E testing, but catches implementation bugs.

2. **Legacy aliases are bug magnets.** When someone copies an endpoint and makes a "legacy alias," they often miss one detail from the original. Always diff the alias against the primary.
